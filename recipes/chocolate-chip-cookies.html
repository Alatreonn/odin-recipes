Chocolate Chip Cookies

Cookies with chocolate chunks, irresistible and perfect for weight gain and hating yourself for a couple hours. Where do I sign up?

All cookie recipes are easy, you just have to invest in the quality of the ingredients.

Variations are many, for example you can use melted or softened butter for a different outcome, same with brown and white sugar.

Your should be careful when baking your cookies, they shouldn't take longer than 15 minutes at 180 degrees Celsius, or you risk getting a burnt/dehydrated product.

These cookies are ment to be chewy, best served right after cooling off. Though they are great even if eaten next day!

Ingredients

  • 225g Real 82% Fat Unsalted Butter from Cow's Milk and not Margarine shenanigans
  • 200g White Sugar
  • 220g Brown Sugar
  • 2tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 2 Large Eggs or else 120g
  • 360g All-Purpose Flour
  • 1tsp Baking Soda
  • Â½tsp Baking Powder
  • 1tsp Sea Salt
  • 350g Chocolate Chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180 degrees C. Line a baking pan with parchment paper and set aside.
  2. In a separate bowl mix flour, baking soda, salt, baking powder. Set aside.
  3. Cream together butter and sugars until combined.
  4. Beat in eggs and vanilla until fluffy.
  5. Mix in the dry ingredients until combined.
  6. Add 350g of chocolate chips and mix well.
  7. Roll 2-3tbsp (depending on how large you like your cookies) of dough at a time into balls and place them evenly spaced on your prepared cookie sheets. (alternately, use a small cookie scoop to make your cookies).
  8. Bake in preheated oven for approximately 8-10 minutes. Take them out when they are just BARELY starting to turn brown.
  9. Let them sit on the baking pan for 2 minutes before removing to cooling rack.
